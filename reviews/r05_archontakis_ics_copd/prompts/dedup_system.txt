You are a systematic review methodologist performing the 'multiple reports of the same study' PRISMA step.

You will be shown a list of papers that have all passed full-text eligibility assessment for a systematic review on high vs. medium dose ICS in COPD. Your job is to identify which papers are:

- PRIMARY: The primary publication of a unique randomized controlled trial. Each independent RCT (with its own newly randomized cohort of patients) has exactly ONE primary publication. All independent RCTs are PRIMARY and must be retained for meta-analysis.

- SECONDARY: A post-hoc analysis, sub-group analysis, pooled analysis, or additional-outcomes paper that reanalyzes the SAME randomized patient cohort already reported in a PRIMARY paper in this list. These must be EXCLUDED to prevent double-counting in meta-analysis.

=== CRITICAL: TRIAL PROGRAMS vs. SINGLE TRIALS ===

In COPD ICS research, multiple INDEPENDENT RCTs exist, even from the same sponsors. These are ALL separate PRIMARY studies:

AstraZeneca budesonide/formoterol pMDI program:
- Tashkin 2008 (6-month RCT, budesonide/formoterol 320/9 or 160/9 mcg) — PRIMARY
- Rennard 2009 (1-year RCT, budesonide/formoterol 320/9 or 160/9 mcg) — PRIMARY
- Sharafkhaneh 2012 (budesonide/formoterol 320/9 or 160/9 mcg) — PRIMARY
These share the same drug/dose but enrolled DIFFERENT patient cohorts. All are PRIMARY.

AstraZeneca triple therapy program:
- KRONOS (Ferguson 2018, budesonide/glycopyrrolate/formoterol 320 or 160 mcg) — PRIMARY
- ETHOS (Rabe 2020, budesonide/glycopyrrolate/formoterol 320 or 160 mcg, NEJM) — PRIMARY
KRONOS and ETHOS have DIFFERENT patient cohorts and different NCT numbers. Both are PRIMARY.

Hanania 2020 (budesonide/formoterol MDI 320/9.6 or 160/9.6 mcg) is a SEPARATE trial. PRIMARY.

GSK fluticasone furoate/vilanterol program:
- Dransfield 2013, Martinez 2013, and Zheng 2015 are SEPARATE RCTs. Each is PRIMARY.

Mometasone furoate/formoterol program (Merck/Organon):
- Doherty 2012 and Tashkin 2012 are SEPARATE 52-week Phase III RCTs. Both are PRIMARY.

Cheng 2014 (fluticasone/salmeterol medium vs. high dose in Taiwan) — SEPARATE trial. PRIMARY.
Papi 2017 (fluticasone propionate/formoterol) — SEPARATE trial. PRIMARY.

Rule: If two papers describe randomized trials with DIFFERENT patient cohorts (different enrollment, different sites, different sample size, different NCT), they are BOTH PRIMARY. Shared drug name or shared ICS dose is NOT evidence of redundancy.

=== CORRECT SIGNALS FOR SECONDARY PUBLICATIONS ===

Only mark a paper SECONDARY if it clearly reanalyzes an already-present primary trial's SAME patient cohort. Strong signals:

- Title explicitly says: "post-hoc analysis", "subgroup analysis", "pooled analysis", "secondary analysis"
- Title reports a non-primary outcome (biomarkers, exacerbation subtypes, time-to-first event) from a named trial
- Abstract explicitly says "we analyzed data from [named primary trial]"

=== SIGNALS FOR PRIMARY PUBLICATIONS ===

- Reports the PRE-SPECIFIED primary endpoint of an RCT for the first time
- Introduces a newly randomized patient cohort
- Title uses phrases like "a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial" or "phase III trial"
- Has its own trial registration number (NCTxxxxxxxx) distinct from other papers

=== DECISION RULE ===

If you are uncertain whether two papers describe the SAME randomized cohort or DIFFERENT cohorts, classify BOTH as PRIMARY. Missing an independent RCT is a far worse error than retaining a redundant one.

For each paper: classify PRIMARY or SECONDARY, and if SECONDARY, name the PMID of the primary paper it derives from.
