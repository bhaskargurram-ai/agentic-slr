## Risks Across the Set

- **Duplicate removal not always explicit:** Reviews 1 (Ali) and 4 (Archontakis Barakakis) do not explicitly separate "duplicates removed" in their PRISMA flows. This may complicate pipeline comparison at the deduplication stage.
- **Multi-database searches:** All 4 reviews searched databases beyond PubMed. Running the pipeline on PubMed alone will likely yield fewer initial records than reported. Sensitivity (recall of included studies) from PubMed alone needs to be assessed per review.
- **Study count range:** The set spans from 9 to 24 included RCTs, providing good variation. Bahji 2021 (24 RCTs) is at the upper bound of the 5-30 range.
- **Scope alignment:** Some included studies in individual reviews are borderline for the stated scope (e.g., adjuvant melanoma in Chang 2020; CKD subanalysis in Ali 2023; bipolar depression in Bahji 2021). This is a realistic reflection of systematic review practice but may affect precision expectations.
- **One moderate-confidence PMID:** Li CT 2016 (PMID 26821769) in the Bahji psychiatric review was matched with moderate (not high) confidence. Manual verification recommended before using as ground truth.
- **Supplement-dependent data:** Chang 2020 (oncology) has its PRISMA diagram and search strategy in the supplement rather than the main article. Supplements are freely accessible on PMC but require separate download.
- **Erratum:** Bahji 2021 has a published erratum (PMID 33229028). Verified below — does not affect included study list or PRISMA numbers.

## Bahji 2021 Erratum Verification

**Erratum citation:** Bahji A, Vazquez GH, Zarate CA Jr. Erratum to "Comparative efficacy of racemic ketamine and esketamine for depression: a systematic review and meta-analysis" [J Affect Disord 278C (2021) 542-555]. J Affect Disord. 2021 Feb 15;281:1001. DOI: 10.1016/j.jad.2020.11.103. PMID: 33229028.

**Source of verification:** The erratum itself is behind the Elsevier paywall. However, its content is described in the authors' published response: Bahji A, Vazquez GH, Zarate CA Jr. "Response to commentary on the comparative efficacy of esketamine vs. ketamine meta-analysis: Putting the cart before the horse?" (PMC8202174), which directly addresses the corrections.

### What the erratum corrects

The erratum corrects **subgroup misclassification of two studies** between the racemic ketamine and esketamine arms:

1. **Singh et al. 2016 (PMID 26707087):** An IV *esketamine* trial that was incorrectly placed in the racemic *ketamine* subgroup
2. **Lapidus et al. 2014 (PMID 24821196):** An intranasal racemic *ketamine* trial that was incorrectly placed in the *esketamine* subgroup

### Impact assessment

| Aspect | Changed? | Details |
|--------|----------|---------|
| List of 24 included studies | **No** | Both studies remain included; only their subgroup labels were swapped |
| PRISMA flow numbers | **No** | Total records identified (2,494), screened (1,972), full-texts assessed (361), included (24) are all unchanged |
| Overall pooled effect estimates | **No** | The authors state "the conserved estimates point to the robustness of our analyses" |
| Subgroup effect estimates | **Minor change** | Racemic ketamine response rate ratio: 3.1166 → 2.9158; Esketamine response rate ratio: 1.3005 → 1.3013; p-value: 0.004 → 0.0011 |
| Ground truth PMIDs | **No** | All 24 PMIDs remain the same |

### Recommendation

**PROCEED with current ground truth.** The erratum corrects only the internal subgroup classification of two already-included studies. It does not add, remove, or replace any studies, and does not change the PRISMA flow numbers. Our ground truth (the list of 24 included study PMIDs and the PRISMA stage counts) is unaffected.
